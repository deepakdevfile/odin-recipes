Aloo Gobi Recipe

Make authentic homestyle Aloo Gobi with this easy step by step recipe. This is the kind of aloo gobi I grew up eating on everyday basis- made with simple ingredients, hearty and super comforting, especially when paired with a bowl of jeera rice and my favorite dal tadka. In my opinion, this Indian potato and cauliflower dish perfectly encapsulates what Indian home cooking is all about.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium head Cauliflower (cut into bite-sized florets)
  • 2 medium Potatoes (peeled and cubed into similar sizes as the cauliflower)
  • 1 large Onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 medium Tomatoes (finely chopped or pureed)
  • 1-inch Ginger (finely julienned or minced)
  • 2-3 cloves Garlic (minced)
  • 1-2 Green chilies (slit lengthwise)
  • Fresh coriander/cilantro leaves (chopped, for garnish)
  • 3 tbsp Cooking oil or ghee
  • 1 tsp Cumin seeds (jeera)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric powder (haldi)
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder (for warmth and color)
  • 1.5 tsp Coriander powder (dhaniya)
  • 1/2 tsp Garam masala
  • 1 tsp Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek leaves, crushed between your palms)
  • Salt to taste
  • A squeeze of fresh lemon juice

Steps

  1. Soak your cauliflower florets in warm salted water for 5 minutes to clean them thoroughly, then drain. To speed up cooking and ensure even textures, you can optionally pan-fry the potato cubes and cauliflower florets in 1 tbsp of oil for 5 minutes until lightly golden. Set them aside.
  2. Heat the remaining oil or ghee in a large pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for 30 seconds. Add the chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent and soft.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for 1 minute until fragrant. Add the chopped tomatoes along with a pinch of salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften, break down, and the oil begins to separate from the edges of the mixture.
  4. Turn the heat down to low. Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, and Kashmiri red chili powder. Stir quickly for about 30 to 60 seconds to toast the spices without burning them.
  5. Add the potatoes and cauliflower to the pan. Gently toss them so they are completely coated in the spiced masala wrapper. Sprinkle about 2-3 tablespoons of water, cover tightly with a lid, and let it cook on low heat. Stir gently every few minutes to prevent sticking. Cook until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  6. Remove the lid. Sprinkle the garam masala, crushed kasuri methi, and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice. Give it one final, gentle stir. Garnish generously with fresh coriander and serve hot with rotis, parathas, or steaming rice.
